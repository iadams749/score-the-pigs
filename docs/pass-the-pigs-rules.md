# Pass the Pigs — Rules

Source: [Wikipedia — Pass the Pigs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pass_the_Pigs)

## Overview

Pass the Pigs is a push-your-luck dice game played with two small model pigs
instead of dice. Each pig has a dot on one side. On a turn, a player throws both
pigs and scores points based on how they land. The player may keep rolling to
add to their turn score, or stop and bank the accumulated points onto their game
total before passing the pigs to the next player.

**Winning:** the first player to reach a predetermined total score — usually
**100 points** — wins.

## Turn flow

1. The player throws both pigs.
2. The way the pigs land determines the result:
   - A **scoring position** adds points to the player's turn score. The player
     may then either roll again or stop and bank the turn score.
   - A **Pig Out** ends the turn immediately and the turn score is lost.
   - An **Oinker (Makin' Bacon)** ends the turn and resets the player's _game
     total_ to zero.
   - A **Piggyback** eliminates the player from the game.
3. When the player stops voluntarily, the turn score is added to their game
   total and play passes to the next player.

## Scoring positions

### Single pig positions

When one pig lands in a special position (and the other lies plainly on its
side), the roll scores:

| Position       | Description                                        | Points |
| -------------- | -------------------------------------------------- | ------ |
| Razorback      | Pig lies on its back                               | 5      |
| Trotter        | Pig stands on all four legs                        | 5      |
| Snouter        | Pig balances on its snout and front legs           | 10     |
| Leaning Jowler | Pig rests on its snout, one ear, and one front leg | 15     |

### Both pigs on their sides

| Result  | Description                                                       | Points                        |
| ------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------- |
| Sider   | Both pigs lie on the **same** side (both dot up or both dot down) | 1                             |
| Pig Out | Pigs lie on **opposite** sides (one dot up, one dot down)         | Turn score is lost; turn ends |

### Doubles

When both pigs land in the same special position, the score is doubled beyond
the sum of the two:

| Result                | Points |
| --------------------- | ------ |
| Double Razorback      | 20     |
| Double Trotter        | 20     |
| Double Snouter        | 40     |
| Double Leaning Jowler | 60     |

### Mixed combinations

When the two pigs land in _different_ special positions (e.g. one Trotter and
one Snouter), the roll scores the **sum of the individual values**
(5 + 10 = 15 in that example).

## Penalty positions

| Result                | Description                                              | Consequence                                 |
| --------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------- |
| Pig Out               | Pigs on opposite sides                                   | Turn score set to 0; turn ends              |
| Oinker / Makin' Bacon | Both pigs touching each other while resting on the table | Player's **game total** set to 0; turn ends |
| Piggyback             | One pig comes to rest on top of the other                | Player is **eliminated** from the game      |

## Variant: Hog Call

Once a player has scored at least 20 points, the non-throwing players may guess
the thrower's upcoming roll by calling "sooee!". A correct guess earns the
caller double the points of that roll; an incorrect guess costs them double.
